New-York

I have a lot of extended family and friends that live in New-York, so every year for thanksgiving my family decided to celebrate in New-York. New-York is best known for its busy city atmosphere and its eclectic architecture. New York, often called New York City (NYC) to distinguish it from the State of New York, is the most populous city in the United States.

Popular tourist attractions:

  • Niagra Falls Waterfall
  • Statue of Liberty
  • Empire State Building
  • Central Park
  • Brooklyn Bridge

Popular Foods:

  • New-York Style Pizza
  • Bagels
  • Street-Food
  • Blackout Cake

Florida

Ive visited Florida once or twice during my summer breaks in school. Florida is best known for its Disney World location in Orlando and for its tropical "vacation-esque" atmosphere. Florida is a Peninsula- meaning its almost completely surrounded by water. Its northernost edge is connected to Alabama in the northwest and Georgia in the northeast. Take a swim off Florida's west coast and you'll be in the Gulf of Mexico.

Popular tourist attractions:

  • Disney World
  • Florida Caverns
  • Siesta Beach
  • Discovery Cove
  • Coral Castle

Popular Foods:

  • Key lime pie
  • Conch Fritters
  • Craft Beer
  • Florida Stone Crab
  • Gator Tail/Gator Bites

Texas

Though my time in texas was cut short by covid, I think that Texas was one of my favorite states in the U.S. Texas is best known for its cowboy culture, which is a large part of the state's history and heritage. Cowboys were originally cattle herders who drove their herds from Texas to markets in other states. You can still see them riding horses in the Texan countryside. Today, cowboy culture is celebrated in many ways, including rodeos, country music, and cowboy hats and boots.

Popular tourist attractions:

  • NASA Space Center
  • Big Bend National Park
  • The Alamo
  • Natural Bridge Caverns
  • Fort Worth Water Gardens

Popular Foods:

  • Texas Barbecue
  • Chicken-fried Stake
  • Kolaches
  • Chilis and Stews
